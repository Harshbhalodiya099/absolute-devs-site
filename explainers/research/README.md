# Research briefs

One brief per future explainer. These are **pre-writing documents**: first
principles, best existing resources, interaction/story directions, and a
creative brief. They deliberately do not contain narration, scenes, or code.

Every "should be interactive" recommendation is calibrated to the engine's
actual interaction model: **params → re-run `build()` → re-simulate**. The
strongest interactions are ones where changing a knob changes the *system's
story*, not just a number on screen.

## The reusable research process (for any future topic)

1. **First principles** — why the concept exists, what failed before it, the
   one-sentence core intuition, the #1 misconception, what's hard to visualize.
2. **Best resources** — only outstanding ones; for each, *what it teaches
   better than anyone* and which single move to steal the principle from.
3. **Interactive inspiration** — sims, games, tools; what to learn, what NOT
   to copy.
4. **Story directions** — 3–5 framings (mystery, failure-first, simulation…),
   each with audience, strengths, weaknesses. Pick later, not here.
5. **Learning psychology** — which techniques fit *this* topic and why.
6. **Aha moments** — where confusion → insight naturally flips; make each one
   a param or a prediction-before-reveal.
7. **Visual opportunities** — animate / interact / simulate / keep static.
8. **Common tutorial mistakes** — and the explicit counter-move.
9. **Design inspiration** — motion, info-vis, storytelling principles.
10. **Creative brief** — one page: intuition, objectives (educational AND
    emotional), misconception, analogy, interactions, story direction, risks.
